Study Strategy & Tips

🧪 Organic Chem — Fixing Your Weak Spot

  • Understand mechanism first — GOC is the master key to all reactions
  • Make a 1-page "Named Reactions" sheet: Aldol, Cannizzaro, Clemmensen, Reimer-Tiemann, Kolbe…
  • After each chapter, do 10–15 PYQs before moving on
  • Day 7 of Week 1 is Organic revision day — don't skip it

🪨 Inorganic — NCERT is the Bible

  • Most inorganic JEE/COMEDK Qs are directly lifted from NCERT
  • Make trend tables for p-block (acidity, basicity, oxidation states)
  • Coordination: IUPAC naming + isomerism types + VBT/CFT concepts
  • Colour code notes by block — visual memory helps a lot

⚛️ Physics — High Yield Topics

  • Mechanics = 30–35% of JEE Adv Physics → master it absolutely
  • Electrostatics + Magnetism = biggest COMEDK chunk → do thoroughly
  • Modern Physics (photoelectric, Bohr, radioactivity) is scoring — easy marks
  • Solve last 5 years JEE Adv Physics papers for question patterns

📐 Maths — Speed is Key for BITSAT

  • Calculus (limits, integration) = biggest chapter, most questions across all 3 exams
  • Coordinate geometry = easiest scoring, prioritize for quick marks
  • Probability + PnC appear every year in JEE Adv — Bayes theorem is must
  • For BITSAT: practice 150Q in under 3 hrs at least twice before exam

🕐 Ideal Daily Schedule

  • 6:30–8:00 AM — Hardest topic (fresh brain = deep work)
  • 9:00–11:30 AM — Theory + worked examples
  • 3:00–5:30 PM — Problems + PYQ practice
  • 7:00–8:00 PM — Formulae/concept quick review
  • No phone after 9 PM. Sleep by 10:30 PM minimum.

🎯 Exam-Specific Rules

  • COMEDK: No negative marking → attempt ALL 180 questions, guess if unsure
  • JEE Adv: Read marking scheme carefully, partial marks available in some Qs
  • BITSAT: 150Q + 12 bonus Qs at end — speed drills are non-negotiable
  • Never study new material the evening before any exam

📝 Mock Test Strategy

  • Week 4: 2× full COMEDK mocks (180Q, strictly timed 3 hrs)
  • Week 5: 1× JEE Adv mock — attempt both papers in one day
  • After every mock: analyse wrong answers before checking solutions
  • Maintain error log — note which chapter each mistake belongs to

🧠 Memory & Retention

  • Spaced repetition: revisit a topic after 1 day, 3 days, 1 week
  • Teach it out loud — if you can't explain it simply, you don't know it
  • Write formula sheets by hand, not typed — motor memory is real
  • 7+ hrs sleep is non-negotiable — memory consolidates only during sleep
